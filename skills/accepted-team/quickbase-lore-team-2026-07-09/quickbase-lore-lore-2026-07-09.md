# Quickbase Lore & Legends

*(Entirely unofficial. Do not cite in a board meeting.)*

## The Legend of the First App

Legend has it that the very first Quickbase app ever built wasn't a business tool at all — it was a tracker for who had taken the last cup of coffee without starting a new pot. It allegedly had 14 fields, 3 relationships, and one very passive-aggressive report called "Offenders (This Week)."

## The Qrew Naming Ceremony

Nobody agrees on who first called the community "the Qrew." Popular theory #1: it started as a typo in a Slack message that nobody corrected because it looked too good. Popular theory #2: someone just really wanted an excuse to make a pirate joke in a company-wide email. Both theories are treated as equally credible, which is to say, not very.

## The Sacred Pipeline Report

There is a company legend about a sales pipeline report that was so beautifully built, so perfectly formatted, that it was allegedly never touched again out of fear of breaking it. Supposedly it's still running today, quietly, like a shrine nobody dares clean.

## The Great Naming Convention Schism

Somewhere in Quickbase history, there was reportedly a multi-week internal debate over whether table names should be singular or plural ("Customer" vs "Customers"). No official record of a winner exists. Both camps still believe they won.

## The Mythical "Fix It In No-Code" Button

Every builder has, at some point, wished for a big red button labeled "Fix It In No-Code" that solves every broken relationship and orphaned record with one click. It does not exist. It has never existed. And yet the phrase lives on as the unofficial company motto for "we'll figure it out."

## The Automation That Achieved Sentience (Allegedly)

There's a long-running joke about one automation, built years ago, so deeply nested with conditional logic that nobody who inherited it has ever fully understood what it does — only that turning it off breaks something, somewhere, that nobody can identify. It has been nicknamed things ranging from "The Oracle" to "Voldemort" depending on who you ask.

## The Empower Conference Curse

Minor company superstition: whoever jokes about "nothing going wrong this year" at Empower is single-handedly responsible for the AV system failing during the keynote. No one takes credit for starting this superstition. Everyone still knocks on wood.
